## Bill Negotiation Services

Services that negotiate on your behalf (typically 30-50% of first year savings):

| Service | Fee | Success Rate | Best For |
|---------|-----|--------------|----------|
| Rocket Money | 30-40% of savings | ~80% | Internet, cable, phone |
| BillShark | 40% of savings | ~85% | Internet, cable, phone, medical |
| Truebill | 40% of savings | ~75% | Internet, cable |

## Services WHERE NEGOTIATION WORKS

### Internet Providers (High Success)

| Provider | Typical Savings | Approach |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| **Xfinity/Comcast** | $200-400/yr | Retention dept, mention competitor pricing |
| **AT&T Internet** | $150-300/yr | Loyalty discounts, bundle offers |
| **Spectrum** | $100-250/yr | New customer promos for existing |
| **Cox** | $150-300/yr | Retention deals common |
| **Frontier** | $100-200/yr | Often flexible on pricing |

**Script:** "I'm looking at my bill and competitors are offering better rates. I'd like to stay but need you to match [competitor rate] or I'll have to switch."

### Cable/Satellite TV (Moderate Success)

| Provider | Typical Savings | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| **DirecTV** | $200-400/yr | Retention offers common |
| **Dish Network** | $150-300/yr | Will match competitors |
| **Xfinity TV** | $100-300/yr | Bundle discounts available |

### Mobile Phone (Moderate Success)

| Carrier | What's Negotiable | What's Not |
|---------|------------------|------------|
| **AT&T Wireless** | Loyalty discounts, autopay discounts | Plan pricing |
| **Verizon** | Military/first responder/teacher discounts | Plan pricing |
| **T-Mobile** | Perks, add-on fees | Generally fixed plans |

### Utilities (Varies by Region)

- **Electric/Gas**: Rate plans sometimes negotiable, demand response programs
- **Trash/Water**: Rarely negotiable (municipal)
- **Home Security**: ADT, SimpliSafe often negotiate

### Medical Bills (High Success, Different Process)

- 30-40% of medical bills contain errors
- Negotiate BEFORE paying, not after
- Request itemized bills, challenge inflated charges
- Payment plans often available at 0% interest

## Alternative Strategies for Non-Negotiables

When negotiation won't work, try these:

### 1. Annual Billing
Typical savings: 15-20%
- Most subscriptions discount annual vs monthly
- Example: Spotify $10.99/mo = $131.88/yr vs $99/yr annual = $33 savings

### 2. Student/Military/Teacher Discounts
- Adobe: 60% off for students
- Spotify: 50% off for students
- Amazon Prime: 50% off for students
- Apple Music: 50% off for students

### 3. Family/Shared Plans
- Split costs with family members
- Many services allow 5-6 users
- Example: Spotify Family $16.99/6 = $2.83/person

### 4. Downgrade Tier
- Do you need 4K? (Netflix Premium → Standard)
- Do you need all features? (Adobe All Apps → Single App)

### 5. Cancel and Resubscribe
- New customer promos often better than loyalty
- Wait 30 days, sign up with new email/payment
- Works well for: streaming, news subscriptions

### 6. Bundle Existing Services
- Disney+/Hulu/ESPN+ bundle saves vs separate
- Apple One bundles multiple Apple services
- T-Mobile/Verizon include streaming perks

## Key Insight

**Negotiation works when:**
- Company has retention department
- Competitive market (can switch providers)
- Service is localized (internet, cable, phone)

**Negotiation doesn't work when:**
- Standardized global pricing
- No retention department exists
- Service has no competitors (unique offering)

Don't waste time calling Netflix or Spotify to negotiate - use alternatives instead.
